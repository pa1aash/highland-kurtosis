## Notes

1. **X0 areal density (line 313):** Corrected from 43 to 39 g/cm². With density 1.24 g/cm³ and X0 = 315 mm = 31.5 cm, the areal density is 1.24 × 31.5 = 39.06 g/cm².

2. **Figure generation pipeline:** The 7 paper figures (`figure_{1-7}_*.png`) are generated by `make_all_figures.py` (figures 2-7) and `make_figure1.py` (figure 1) using **synthetic data**, not actual Geant4 ROOT output. When simulations are rerun, the figures should be regenerated from real data using the analysis scripts (`proposal_analysis.py`, `proposal_figures.py`, etc.) and/or the make scripts updated to use ROOT files.

3. **All \includegraphics paths are correct.** All 7 figures exist in `paper/` at the exact paths specified. No fixes needed.

4. **Thin-wall parametrisation figure (2026-03-23):** New figure `paper/figure_thin_wall_parametrisation.{png,pdf}` generated by `analysis/analyze_thin_wall.py`. Shows κ_M vs ℓ/X₀ at 2/4/6 GeV with logarithmic fits. Not yet referenced in main.tex. Results:
   - Fit: κ_M = a + b·ln(ℓ/X₀) with χ²/ndf ≈ 0.4–1.3
   - 2 GeV: a=2.61, b=−0.44; 4 GeV: a=3.10, b=−0.37; 6 GeV: a=3.27, b=−0.34
   - κ_M(0.4 mm)/κ_M(10 mm) enhancement: 1.34 (2 GeV), 1.30 (4 GeV), 1.26 (6 GeV)
   - Binary lattice predictions increase by +17% to +49% when using thin-wall κ_M
   - Full results in `data/phase01_thin_wall/thin_wall_results.json`
   - Recomputed Table 1 predictions in `data/phase01_thin_wall/thin_wall_recomputed_table1.json`

5. **Model comparison figure (2026-03-23, updated Session 12):** Figure `paper/figure_model_comparison.{png,pdf}` regenerated by `analysis/analyze_model_comparison.py` with corrected rect40 geometry and corrected κ_geo extraction formula. Not yet referenced in main.tex.
   - **κ_geo extraction fixed:** Was `κ_total − κ_M` (simple subtraction, ignores cross-term). Now `κ_geo = (κ_total − κ_M)/(1 + κ_M/3)` from the universal equation, properly isolating the geometric contribution.
   - Solid control κ_M: opt0 (Urban) = 4.93±0.26, opt3 = 1.99±0.20, opt4 (EMZ) = 4.74±0.25
   - rect40 κ_total: opt0 = 16.36±0.97, opt3 = 10.22±0.87, opt4 = 15.92±1.01
   - κ_geo (corrected): opt0 = 4.32±0.38, opt3 = 4.95±0.54, opt4 = 4.34±0.40
   - **All three options now agree within ~1σ** (spread=0.63, mean=4.54±0.36), confirming κ_geo is model-independent
   - Old simple subtraction gave spread=3.19 — the cross-term was responsible for the apparent model dependence
   - Full results in `results/model_comparison_results.json`

6. **Thin-wall correction replaces +/-12% systematic (line 598):** The thin-wall κ_M at 0.4 mm wall thickness gives a first-principles correction: κ_M(0.4mm) ≈ 5.5–5.7 vs κ_M(10mm) ≈ 4.1–4.5, an enhancement of ~26–34% depending on energy. The corrected predictions for binary lattices use κ = (3 + κ_M(0.4mm)) / f − 3 instead of κ = (3 + κ_M(10mm)) / f − 3.

7. **Cut robustness study (2026-03-23):** New figure `paper/figure_cut_robustness.{png,pdf}` generated by `analysis/cut_variation_study.py`. 3-panel plot showing excess kurtosis stability under variation of energy cut fraction, angle cut (Nσ_Highland), and fiducial |x| cut for rect 40% 4 GeV (45601 events). Not yet referenced in main.tex. Results:
   - **Energy cut fraction** (0.85–0.98): κ stable at ~13.3–13.6, no saturation (all within 2×SE of default)
   - **Angle cut** (5–20 σ_H): κ strongly depends on angle cut; saturation at 5σ (κ=6.7 vs 13.6 at default 10σ). Tighter angle cuts truncate tails and suppress kappa. Looser cuts include outliers and inflate kappa (59.0 at 20σ).
   - **Fiducial |x| cut** (3–10 mm): κ stable at ~13.5 for |x|≤5 mm; saturation at 7 mm (κ=16.5) as edge effects enter
   - Full results in `results/cut_variation_results.json`

8. **Systematic uncertainty table (2026-03-23, updated Session 12):** File `paper/table_systematics.tex` regenerated by `analysis/systematic_uncertainty.py`. LaTeX table with 7 systematic sources (was 8) for rect 40% 4 GeV. Not yet \input in main.tex.
   - **Highland log correction REMOVED:** Was δκ=3.05, but (a) double-counts with thin-wall correction (Geant4 sim already includes Highland log physics) and (b) is a fractional variance correction misinterpreted as absolute kurtosis shift.
   - **Angle cut systematic redefined:** Was max-deviation 10σ→15σ (δκ=8.52, dominated budget). Now half-spread |κ(13σ)−κ(7σ)|/2 = 4.32. The old method conflated real Molière-tail physics with systematic uncertainty.
   - Remaining entries: Geant4 MCS model (δκ=4.87), fiducial (δκ=3.11), thin-wall κ_M (δκ=1.32), energy cut (δκ=0.37), beam profile (δκ=0.10), voxelisation (δκ=0.05)
   - **Total systematic = 7.35 (was 10.83), total combined = 7.47 (was 10.91)**
   - Full budget in `results/systematic_budget.json`

9. **Phase 3 stacked-layer N-scaling with Geant4 (2026-03-23, updated):** Analysis now extracts geometric kurtosis κ_geo = (κ_total − κ_M) / (1 + κ_M/3) using κ_M=3.88 from solid control (`data/proposal/control_solid_4GeV.root`). Figure `paper/figure_6_n_scaling_with_geant4.{png,pdf}` plots κ_geo vs N.
   - **Data regenerated:** ROOT files now use corrected cellSize=3.79mm (verified mean_pla≈2.05mm).
   - Geant4 κ_total: N=1: 44.98, N=2: 31.36, N=4: 23.88, N=10: 28.92, N=20: 25.42
   - Geant4 κ_geo:   N=1: 17.93, N=2: 11.99, N=4: 8.73,  N=10: 10.93, N=20: 9.40
   - 1/N scaling still NOT observed — κ_geo collapse ratio diverges (1.0 → 10.5 at N=20). Non-monotonic behaviour (N=10 > N=4) suggests kurtosis dominated by Molière-tail outliers at 64k events.
   - Ray-trace 20% infill data shows clean 1/N scaling (κ=11.97 at N=1 to 0.13 at N=100)
   - Figure 6 two-panel: left = log-log κ vs N (ray-trace + Geant4 + theory), right = universality collapse κN/κ_single
   - **Action needed:** Regenerate data with corrected macros (`scripts/run_stacked_layers.sh`), then rerun analysis

11. **Table 2 stale values (Session 12 audit):** 8 of 20 kappa_geo entries in Table 2 (lines 524–547) do not match `data/sweep0/sweep0_summary.json`. The paper's f_hit and kappa_geo are self-consistent via 3(1−f)/f, but use **stale f_hit values** from an older ray-trace run. Affected: rectilinear 60%/80%, honeycomb 60%/80%, cubic 60%/80%, gyroid 80%, voronoi 80%. **Deferred to Session 19** for Table 2 update.

12. **Angle cut convention finding (Session 12 audit):** Table 1 values (from `proposal_analysis.py`) use Highland sigma from the **actual mean pla_path** — giving a tighter angle cut for lattice geometries (κ≈8.65 for rect40). The systematic budget and `cut_variation_study.py` use Highland sigma from the **fixed 10mm solid** thickness (κ≈13.65 for the same data). Both are valid conventions; the systematic budget is self-consistent within its own convention. For the solid control, both conventions give κ=3.62 (since pla_path=10mm for all events).

13. **κ_geo extraction in Phase 1 results (Session 12 note):** The `analyze_phase1.py` script likely uses the old simple subtraction `κ_geo = κ_total − κ_M`. Should be updated to `κ_geo = (κ_total − κ_M)/(1 + κ_M/3)` for consistency with the corrected `analyze_model_comparison.py`. This would affect Si and muon κ_geo values in note 10.
